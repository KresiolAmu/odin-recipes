Sinigang Recipe

Description:

Sinigang is a classic Filipino soup known for its savory and tangy flavor, usually made with pork, shrimp, or fish, simmered with tamarind (or other souring agents) and mixed with fresh vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly or ribs (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack tamarind soup base (or fresh tamarind if available)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 pcs long green chili (siling haba)
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach)
  • 1 cup sitaw (string beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • Salt or fish sauce (patis) to taste

Steps:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add pork, onion, and tomatoes. Simmer until the pork is tender (about 45–60 minutes).
  2. Add the radish, eggplant, and string beans. Cook for about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the tamarind soup base (or fresh tamarind extract). Adjust sourness to taste.
  4. Add the green chili and kangkong. Simmer for another 2–3 minutes.
  5. Season with fish sauce or salt as needed.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice.
