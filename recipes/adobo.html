Adobo Recipe

Description:

Adobo is a classic Filipino dish made by simmering meat (commonly chicken, pork, or a mix) in soy sauce vinegar,garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. It’s savory, slightly tangy, and often enjoyed with rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken (or pork, or a mix) cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, crushed
  • 1 medium onion, sliced (optional)
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns (or 1/2 tsp ground black pepper)
  • 1 cup water (or as needed)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1–2 medium potatoes, cubed (optional)
  • 1 boiled egg (optional, per serving)
  • Salt and sugar to taste

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, pepper, and bay leaves. Marinate the meat for at least 30 minutes (overnight is best).
  2. Heat oil in a pot. Sauté onion (if using) and garlic from the marinade until fragrant.
  3. Add the meat and cook until lightly browned.
  4. Pour in the marinade and add water. Bring to a boil.
  5. Lower the heat and simmer for 30–45 minutes, or until the meat is tender and the sauce slightly thickens.
  6. Add potatoes (if using) and cook until tender.
  7. Adjust seasoning with salt, sugar, or more vinegar/soy sauce depending on taste.
  8. Serve hot with rice, optionally topped with boiled egg.
